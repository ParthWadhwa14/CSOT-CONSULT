## Day 1: Market Research Fundamentals
**Estimated time:** ~1.5 hours

Before consultants can recommend a strategy, they first need to understand the market they are dealing with. This sounds obvious, but many business problems become much easier—or sometimes disappear entirely—once the market is properly understood. Market research helps answer fundamental questions such as: Who are the customers? What are their needs? How large is the opportunity? What are competitors doing? Day 1 introduces the basics of market research and how consultants use publicly available information to quickly build an understanding of an industry before diving into deeper analysis.

## Day 2: Market Sizing, TAM-SAM-SOM & Guesstimates
**Estimated time:** ~2 hours

Once consultants understand a market, the next question is usually whether the opportunity is large enough to matter. Businesses constantly make decisions about which markets to enter, which customer segments to target, and where to invest resources. To answer these questions, consultants often need to estimate market size and potential. The challenge is that perfect information is rarely available. Instead, they rely on logical assumptions, publicly available data, and structured estimation techniques. Day 2 introduces the concepts of market sizing and guesstimates, two skills that appear frequently in consulting projects and case interviews alike.

### Read first:
2. **"TAM, SAM, and SOM: What They Are and How to Calculate Them"**
   * **Source:** HubSpot
   * **Overview:** Introduces three of the most common market sizing concepts used in consulting, venture capital, and strategy roles: Total Addressable Market (TAM), Serviceable Available Market (SAM), and Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM). Focus on understanding what each metric represents and why they become progressively more realistic measures of opportunity.
   * **Title:** TAM, SAM & SOM: What Do They Mean & How Do You Calculate Them?
   * **Link:** https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/tam-sam-som
   * **Estimated time:** 15 minutes

### Then watch:

  * **Source:** YouTube
  * **Overview:** A practical introduction to guesstimates, a structured approach to estimating quantities when complete data is unavailable. Rather than guessing randomly, the video demonstrates how large problems can be broken into smaller assumptions and calculated logically.
  * **Title:** Market-sizing & Guesstimate questions - Not as hard as you think
  * **Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v5eKAmZuQzs&list=WL&index=4

## Day 3: Industry Analysis: SWOT & PESTLE
**Estimated time:** ~2 hours

After understanding the size of a market, consultants try to understand the factors that shape it. A good recommendation depends not only on knowing the opportunity, but also on understanding the environment in which a business operates. Why are some companies successful while others struggle in the same market? What external trends could create opportunities or threats in the future? Day 3 introduces two widely used frameworks that help answer these questions. SWOT focuses on a company's internal strengths and weaknesses as well as external opportunities and threats, while PESTLE provides a structured way to analyze the broader environment affecting an industry.

### Read first:
3. **"What Is SWOT Analysis? How To Do a SWOT Analysis"**
   * **Source:** Forbes Advisor
   * **Overview:** A clear introduction to one of the most commonly used business frameworks. Focus on understanding the distinction between internal factors (strengths and weaknesses) and external factors (opportunities and threats). Pay attention to the examples showing how SWOT can be used to identify strategic priorities rather than simply listing observations.
   * **Title:** What Is A SWOT Analysis? Download Our Free Template – Forbes Advisor

## Day 4: Competitive Strategy: Porter's Five Forces & Value Chain Analysis
**Estimated time:** ~2.5 hours

Not all attractive markets are attractive businesses. Two companies can operate in the same industry and achieve very different results. To understand why, consultants often look beyond market size and examine how competition works within an industry and where value is created inside a business. Day 4 introduces two of the most influential strategy frameworks. Porter's Five Forces helps assess the attractiveness of an industry by analyzing competitive pressures, while Value Chain Analysis helps identify the activities that create value and potentially provide a competitive advantage.

### Read first:
5. **"The Five Forces"**
   * **Source:** Harvard Business School
   * **Overview:** This is one of the clearest explanations of Michael Porter's Five Forces framework. It introduces the five forces that determine industry attractiveness: rivalry among competitors, threat of new entrants, bargaining power of suppliers, bargaining power of buyers, and threat of substitutes. As you read, focus on how these forces affect profitability rather than simply competition.
   * **Title:** The Five Forces - Institute For Strategy And Competitiveness - Harvard Business School
   * **Link:** https://www.isc.hbs.edu/strategy/business-strategy/Pages/the-five-forces.aspx

## Day 5: Bringing the Frameworks Together
**Estimated time:** ~1 hour

Throughout the week, you have been introduced to several frameworks that consultants use to understand markets, industries, and businesses. A common misconception among beginners is that consulting is about memorizing frameworks. In reality, the value comes from knowing when a framework is useful, what question it helps answer, and how different frameworks can complement one another. Day 5 is about stepping back and connecting the pieces. Rather than learning a new tool, the focus is on understanding how consultants choose and apply frameworks depending on the problem in front of them.

### Read:
9. **"Top 7 Strategic Frameworks Every Leader Should Know"**
   * **Source:** Hyper Island
   * **Overview:** A concise overview of several widely used business frameworks, including SWOT, PESTLE, Porter's Five Forces, Business Model Canvas, and McKinsey 7S. Unlike the earlier readings this week, the goal here is not to learn a new framework but to understand what kinds of business questions different frameworks are designed to answer and when each is most useful.
   * **Title:** Top 7 Strategic Frameworks every leader should know
   * **Link:** https://hyperisland.com/en/blog/hyper-insights/top-7-strategic-frameworks-every-leader-should-know
