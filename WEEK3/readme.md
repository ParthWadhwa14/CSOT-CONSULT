# Week 3: Research, Evidence & Insight Generation

**Total estimated time:** ~8–10 hours across 5 days. 
In Weeks 1 and 2, you learned how to structure business problems and analyze the markets surrounding them. This week focuses on gathering and interpreting evidence. You will learn how consultants conduct research, evaluate sources, analyze competitors, extract insights from data, and refine hypotheses as new information emerges.

---

## Day 1: Secondary Research & Source Quality
**Estimated time:** ~1.5 hours

Before consultants analyze a business, they first need reliable information. Most projects begin with secondary research: gathering information from company websites, industry reports, news articles, government publications, and investor materials. The challenge is that not all sources are equally trustworthy.

### Read first:
* **"Secondary Research: Definition, Methods & Examples"** (Qualtrics)
  * A practical introduction to secondary research and the sources consultants commonly use.
  * [Link](https://www.qualtrics.com/experience-management/research/secondary-research/) | 15 minutes
* **"Evaluating Sources"** (Purdue OWL)
  * A simple framework for assessing credibility (who, why, and what evidence).
  * [Link](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/research_and_citation/conducting_research/evaluating_sources_of_information/index.html) | 10 minutes

### Then watch:
* **Video 1: “How To Do Market Research For Consulting”** (Management Consulted)
  * A practical walkthrough of how to build an understanding of an unfamiliar industry efficiently.
  * [Link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rvubOaqGcuo) | 20 minutes

> ### Day 1 Reflection Question
> Imagine you are advising Sarvam AI on whether multilingual AI is a sustainable opportunity in India, without conducting original interviews. What sources would you consult first? Which would you trust most, and which would you treat cautiously? Explain the potential biases and verification methods for each.

## Day 4: From Facts to Insights
**Estimated time:** ~2 hours

### Read first:

## Task Week 3 Deliverable (Submitted for Evaluation)

Using the Sarvam AI problem statement and the tools from this week, produce a **one-page evidence assessment**.

### Requirements (No prose paragraphs):
1. **One clearly stated hypothesis:** A specific claim about which business model Sarvam should prioritize.
2. **5 pieces of evidence:** For each, include:
   * Evidence statement
   * Source
   * Reliability rating (High / Medium / Low)
   * Whether it *Supports* or *Challenges* your hypothesis
3. **One competitor comparison:** Compare Sarvam against one competitor using no more than 4 dimensions.
4. **One insight chain:** Fact → Observation → Insight → Business Implication.
5. **One updated conclusion:** One sentence answering: *"Based on the evidence available today, what appears to be Sarvam’s strongest path to scale?"*

> **Before submitting, check:** Did you distinguish evidence from opinion? Does each piece of evidence directly relate to your hypothesis? Does your insight chain explain *why* a fact matters?
