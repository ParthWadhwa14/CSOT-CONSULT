# Problem Statement
## Sarvam AI: Which Business Model Should It Commit To?

### Before you begin
Sarvam AI is a real company. This document gives you enough to orient yourself, not enough to answer the question. You are expected to research the company, its competitors, its market, and the broader AI commercialisation landscape independently. The quality of your research will determine the quality of your recommendation. Treat this like a first client briefing: it raises more questions than it answers.

### Background
Sarvam AI was founded in August 2023 by Vivek Raghavan and Pratyush Kumar, both from IIT Madras and the AI4Bharat initiative. Raghavan previously worked on the technology architecture of Aadhaar and UPI. Their founding thesis: the world's leading AI systems are built for English speakers, leaving India's 1.4 billion people (the majority of whom speak one of 22 scheduled languages) behind.
Within five months of incorporation, Lightspeed, Peak XV Partners, and Khosla Ventures committed approximately $41 million, one of the fastest large seed rounds in Indian startup history.

### What Sarvam Has Built
Sarvam operates across multiple product layers simultaneously.
* **Foundation models:** Open-source and proprietary LLMs trained for Indian languages, including two flagship models unveiled at the India AI Impact Summit in February 2026, benchmarked competitively against global models on reasoning, coding, and multilingual tasks.
* **Speech and language APIs:** Production-ready APIs for speech-to-text, text-to-speech, and translation across major Indian languages, available on a free tier and pay-per-use basis.
* **Enterprise voice agents:** A platform for deploying multilingual AI agents across phone calls, WhatsApp, and in-app channels, targeting enterprises that serve customers in non-English languages.
* **Vertical applications:** A legal AI workbench covering regulatory research, document drafting, and data extraction.
* **Consumer hardware:** Smart glasses with multilingual voice interaction, planned for 2026.
* **Sovereign AI:** In April 2025, the Indian government selected Sarvam under the IndiaAI Mission (₹10,372 crore national initiative) to build India's first sovereign LLM, with access to government-subsidised NVIDIA GPU compute. Sarvam has also announced state-level partnerships with Odisha and Tamil Nadu.
* **Developer ecosystem:** A startup programme offering API credits, engineering support, and production infrastructure to early-stage companies.

### The Current Situation
Annual revenue as of March 2025: ₹29.1 crore. Team size: approximately 114. In April 2026, Sarvam closed a new round backed by Nvidia, Bessemer, Amazon, and Accel at a valuation roughly 14 times higher than 30 months prior.
Sarvam is simultaneously a government AI partner, an API platform for developers, an enterprise software vendor, a vertical SaaS company, and a consumer hardware company. Each requires a different customer, sales motion, and organisational capability. The government's subsidy is finite. The Indian language performance gap that currently distinguishes Sarvam's models will narrow as global players invest further. The window to convert technical lead into commercial advantage is open, but not indefinitely.

### The Question
Which primary business model should Sarvam commit to over the next three to five years to achieve durable commercial scale, and what would that require it to deprioritise or exit?

### Starting Points for Your Research
* Who is currently paying Sarvam, and for what?
* How have comparable AI companies globally converted language or voice AI capabilities into sustainable businesses?
* What happens to Sarvam's commercial position if the government contract ends or opens to multiple providers?
* Which of Sarvam's capabilities would be genuinely hard for a well-funded global player to replicate in India within 24 months?
* Who are Sarvam's real competitors, and are any of them not obviously AI companies?
